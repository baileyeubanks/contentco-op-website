Brand Positioning

Who we are, why we exist, and who we serve. This is the strategic foundation every piece of creative builds from.

Brand Promise

We make the work visible.

Content Co-op exists to tell the stories of the people and industries that build the real world — energy, construction, manufacturing, infrastructure.

Positioning Statement

For industrial and energy companies who need to show the world what they actually do, Content Co-op is the production partner that embeds with your teams and turns real operations into compelling content — because the work itself is the story.

Core Values

Authenticity over production

Real crews, real sites, real conditions. We don't manufacture moments — we capture them.

Craft over content

Every frame is intentional. We'd rather deliver one exceptional piece than ten mediocre ones.

Partnership over vendor

We embed, we don't just show up. We learn your operations, your people, your language.

Signal over noise

One great piece beats ten mediocre ones. Every deliverable earns its place.

Target Audience

Marketing directors, VP Communications, and brand managers at mid-to-large industrial, energy, and construction companies ($50M-$5B revenue). Decision-makers who understand that their real-world operations are inherently compelling — they just need the right team to capture and tell those stories.

Color Usage Guidelines

Usage Ratio: 70% cream/white backgrounds, 20% ink for text, 10% accent blue for CTAs and highlights.

  • Primary Palette: Cream (#f0ebe0) as background, Ink (#0b1928) as primary text, Accent (#1e4d8c) for interactive elements
  • Supporting Colors: Surface (#faf6ef) for cards, Border (#d8cfc0) for dividers, Ink Secondary (#485670) for supporting text
  • Accessibility: All text/background combinations must meet WCAG AA (4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum). Ink on Cream = 14.2:1. Accent on Cream = 7.1:1.

Never

  • Never use accent blue as a background color
  • Never use cream text on white backgrounds
  • Never introduce new colors outside this palette without owner approval

Clear Space

Minimum clear space around any logo = 1x the height of the spiral mark on all sides. This space must remain free of text, graphics, and page edges.

Minimum Size

  • Digital: 40px height minimum
  • Print: 0.5" height minimum

Approved Backgrounds

  • Warm Cream (#f0ebe0)
  • White (#ffffff)
  • Dark Ink (#0b1928)
  • Photography with sufficient contrast (test at 50% opacity overlay)

Logo Don'ts

  • Never rotate or skew the logo
  • Never apply drop shadows or outer glow
  • Never alter the colors
  • Never place on busy photography without a clear space overlay
  • Never stretch or compress — always maintain original proportions
  • Never outline or add strokes

Photography & Film

Real editorial photography from Content Co-op productions. Every image is from an actual shoot — never stock, never AI-generated.

Visual Mood

Industrial elegance — the beauty in heavy work. Golden hour warmth, natural light preferred. Compositions that find poetry in steel, cable, concrete, and craft.

Composition Rules

  • Rule of thirds, leading lines toward subjects
  • Workers in action, never posed — candid captures of expertise
  • Environmental context — show the scale of the site, the scope of the project
  • Eye-level or slightly low angle — workers as heroes, not subjects

Color Treatment

  • Warm grade, never cold or clinical
  • Slight desaturation of background, subject warmth preserved
  • No HDR, no extreme contrast
  • Consistent across the full shoot — grade the set, not individual frames

Subjects

Real crews, real equipment, real sites. Never stock photography. Always with permission and safety clearance. Subjects should be engaged in actual work, not performing for camera.

● Do

  • Wide establishing shots showing scale
  • Medium shots of hands at work
  • Detail shots of equipment and craft
  • Golden hour and natural light
  • Candid captures of expertise in action

● Don't

  • Posed group photos
  • Stock-style pointing at clipboards
  • Empty facilities without people
  • Logos-only shots with no context
  • Cold, clinical color grades

Motion

Hero motion should be built from real production clips in rotation, not a single slowed loop. The edit and the headline need to describe the same reality on screen.

Motion Guidelines

  • Pacing: Use real clip cadence at native speed. Do not slow footage down for hero playback or the reel will stutter and lose credibility.
  • Rotation: Hero reels should rotate through at least 20 clips so the page feels alive and archival, not like a single repeated moment.
  • Story alignment: Hero headlines must match the footage. If the clips show crews, control rooms, equipment, and field conditions, the copy should say that plainly.
  • Color grading: Warm midtones, lifted shadows, muted highlights. Match the cream/navy palette.
  • Audio: Minimal ambient score or silent for loops. Voiceover only when client-directed.
  • Typography: Fraunces for titles, Plus Jakarta Sans for lower-thirds. White on dark.
  • Loop points: All ambient assets have seamless loop points. Test before deployment.

Voice & Verbal

How Content Co-op speaks, writes, and communicates. These pillars guide every piece of copy we produce.

Brand Personality

Confident but not boastful. Technical but accessible. Warm but not casual. We speak from a place of earned expertise — our work speaks for itself, and our words support that work without overselling it.

Messaging Pillars

Warm

We speak like a trusted partner, not a vendor. Our tone is approachable, human, and grounded. We avoid jargon and corporate distance. Every interaction should feel like a conversation between collaborators who respect each other's time.

Crafted

Every word earns its place. We are precise without being cold, concise without being curt. Our writing reflects the same care we bring to our productions — nothing accidental, nothing wasted. If a sentence doesn't add value, it doesn't ship.

Collaborative

We frame every project as a partnership. "We" over "I." Credit shared generously. We ask questions before we assume. Our language centers the client's goals, not our capabilities. The best work happens when both sides bring something to the table.

Writing Rules

  • Lead with the work, not with us. "We captured 47 hours of refinery footage" becomes "47 hours of refinery footage, captured at shift change."
  • Short sentences. Active voice. Present tense for case studies.
  • Numbers over adjectives. "Award-winning" becomes "3x Telly Award, 2024."
  • Never use: "innovative", "cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art", "solutions", "synergy"

Tone Spectrum

Website

Confident, concise. "Minimal stage, maximum signal." Let the visuals do the heavy lifting. Copy is sparse and precise.

Client Proposals

Warm, specific, evidence-based. Reference past work by name. Include timelines, deliverables, and concrete outcomes.

Social Media

Behind-the-scenes, crew-centric, authentic. Show the process, not just the product. Tag the team. Keep it human.

Internal

Direct, technical, efficient. Skip the pleasantries when speed matters. Use shorthand the team understands.

Do's and Don'ts

● Do

  • Use "we" and "our" to signal partnership
  • Lead with the client's outcome, not your process
  • Write in active voice with concrete details
  • Keep sentences under 25 words when possible
  • Use Title Case for headlines and section headers
  • Reference real work — name projects, cite results
  • End emails with a clear next action
  • Say "I'll follow up by Thursday" instead of "I'll circle back"

● Don't

  • Use buzzwords: "synergy," "leverage," "disrupt," "pivot"
  • Over-promise timelines or scope in writing
  • Use passive voice to avoid accountability
  • Write paragraphs when a bullet list works better
  • Use exclamation points in professional copy (one per email max)
  • Say "touching base" or "just checking in" without substance
  • Use industry jargon with non-production audiences
  • Start sentences with "Actually," "Basically," or "Obviously"

Governance

Brand ownership, approval workflows, and usage guidelines. When in doubt, ask before publishing.

Brand Ownership

  • Bailey Eubanks Owner
    Final authority on all brand decisions
  • All brand assets are proprietary to Content Co-op LLC
  • Modifications to core identity elements (logo, colors, typography) require owner approval

Brand Approval Chain

  • All external-facing creative requires Bailey Eubanks' approval before publication
  • Tier 1 — Self-serve: Using approved templates, selecting from the photo library, applying brand colors to existing layouts
  • Tier 2 — Review required: New creative executions, co-branded materials, press or media usage
  • Tier 3 — Owner approval: Logo modifications, new color introductions, brand voice exceptions, external licensing

Co-Branding Rules

  • CC logo always appears at equal or greater size to partner logos
  • CC spiral mark may be used alone only when the full lockup has been established in the same context
  • Never alter logo proportions, colors, or orientation
  • Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the spiral mark on all sides
  • Never place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds without an overlay
